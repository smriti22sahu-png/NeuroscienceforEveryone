What is Neuroscience?

A simple introduction to the study of the brain and nervous system.

Neuroscience is the scientific study of the brain and the nervous system. It tries to understand how the brain helps us think, learn, remember, feel emotions, and act in the world.

The brain does not work in isolation. It changes as we grow, learn, face challenges, and interact with other people. That is one reason neuroscience is so interesting: it studies both the structure of the brain and the way experience shapes it.

Neuroscience is a broad field. It includes the study of neurons, memory, sleep, attention, movement, and even bigger questions like consciousness and identity.

Why does it matter?

Understanding neuroscience can help us make sense of learning, behavior, brain health, and the experience of being human.
